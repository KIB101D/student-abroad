Erasmus+

How can I apply to the Erasmus +?

Author: Bohdan Holubets

Editor and translator: Sofia Nychyporuk

11/04/2024

Applying to Erasmus+ involves several steps, usually starting at your home university. First, ensure your university has Erasmus+ agreements with institutions abroad in your field of study. Check their international office's website for information on available mobilities, application deadlines, and eligibility criteria (e.g., GPA, language proficiency).

Typically, you'll need to submit an application form, a Learning Agreement outlining courses you'll take abroad, a transcript of records, a motivation letter, a CV, and proof of language proficiency (e.g., IELTS, TOEFL, or a university certificate). Some universities might also require a recommendation letter.

After your home university nominates you, you'll then apply to the host institution, often with similar documents. Once accepted by both, you'll sign a Grant Agreement to receive financial support. Always keep track of deadlines and contact your university's international office for guidance, as procedures can vary.

What level of English will be enough to program of mobility?

Author: Bohdan Holubets

Editor and translator: Sofia Nychyporuk

11/04/2024

First of all, the level of language proficiency must be sufficient to learn and communicate in the language. Usually, the website of the host institution indicates the level that the student should have: it is at least B2, and sometimes C1. The best way to confirm language knowledge is an international certification, such as IELTS or TOEFL. If the program you are applying for is collaborative with your home university, then in some cases you may be able to get this confirmation much faster and easier. It can be a recommendation from your teacher or a certificate from the university necessarily with a signature and seal. All this information should be obtained in advance from the international office of the inviting university. They should advise you and tell you what exactly they accept as proof of language proficiency.

Grants

Is it possible to receive a grant 2 times in a row?

Author: Bohdan Holubets

Editor and translator: Sofia Nychyporuk

05/06/2024

This depends on the specific grant program and its rules. Some grants are designed for one-time participation, while others, especially those with different "levels" or for different study periods (e.g., Bachelor's, Master's, PhD), might allow for multiple applications. For Erasmus+, it's generally possible to receive funding for different mobility periods, provided you meet the eligibility criteria for each new application and stay within the maximum duration for each study cycle (e.g., 12 months per study cycle for Erasmus+). Always check the guidelines of the specific grant and consult with your university's international office or the grant provider.

Can boys cross the border if they are nominated for mobility?

Author: Bohdan Holubets

Editor and translator: Sofia Nychyporuk

11/04/2024

As of the current situation (July 2025) and given the ongoing martial law in Ukraine, there are specific restrictions on the departure of male citizens aged 18 to 60. While student mobility programs like Erasmus+ provide opportunities for study abroad, crossing the border for male students requires adherence to specific legal exceptions and procedures.

Generally, male students who are enrolled in full-time education and are nominated for academic mobility may be eligible to cross the border, but they must have all the necessary documents confirming their student status, nomination for an academic program abroad, and any specific permits required by the State Border Guard Service of Ukraine or other relevant authorities.

It is crucial to verify the most up-to-date regulations with your university's international office, the Ministry of Education and Science of Ukraine, or the State Border Guard Service of Ukraine *before* making any travel plans, as rules can change. Relying solely on past information or general statements may lead to complications at the border. Always ensure you have all required official documents and permissions.
